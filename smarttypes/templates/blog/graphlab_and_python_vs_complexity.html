Everything is related

The universe is a complex place. Everything around us (our weather, economy, back yards, bodies, society, ect...) operate under a cloak of complexity. Humans have a long history of trying to make sense of complex phenomenon. This has been the goal of reductionist science for thousands of years.

"Tiger got to hunt, bird got to fly; Man got to sit and wonder 'why, why, why?' Tiger got to sleep, bird got to land; Man got to tell himself he understand." -- Kurt Vonnegut

Arguably, the butterfly effect is complexity science's crowning achievement (thus far anyway). Show me another complexity inspired movie starring America's man of science, Ashton Kutcher and i'll rethink my claim.

The the butterfly effect is a simple and elegant idea. A butterfly flapping it's wings in Casablanca, Morocco can effect the weather in Cleveland, Ohio. This is an example used to make a larger point. The larger point is: stuff is related.

Perhaps everything is related. If that doesn't melt your mind read what wikipedia has to say about determinism. From August 23 2011: "The philosophical argument here would be that the big bang triggered every single action, and possibly mental thought, through the system of cause and effect."

"everything under the sun is in tune"

Social network analysis

I can't always do it, sometimes though, i can imagine us all (all us crazy humans) as a single interconnected organism. A lot of other people have had this thought, you've probably heard the term 'the human super organism'.

From this perspective human social networks have the same properties as any complex system. The assassination of an Austrian archduke can trigger a world war, just like a butterfly in Casablanca can trigger a Cleveland hail storm.

Like any complex system, predicting the actions of humans is hard. Machines can help.

The visualization below is a Strange attractor, or just attractor. It's a computer generated visualization, of a computer generated model, of a real physical system or process. I'm fond of this image. The model illustrated by this strange attractor uses 3 dimensions. Try to imagine a model w/ a hundred, or a thousand, or a million dimensions. Now you understand why we need computers.

Predicting the market

The stock market is a modern battlefield for computer assisted human analysis.

"Even apart from the instability due to speculation, there is the instability due to the characteristic of human nature that a large proportion of our positive activities depend on spontaneous optimism rather than mathematical expectations." -- John Maynard Keynes

In 2009, computer driven, algorithmic trading firms accounted for 73% of all US equity trading volume.

This is the paper that got me interested in social network analysis: Twitter mood predicts the stock market.

You may have seen this. If not, you may want to read that again. Twitter mood predicts the stock market. That's no small feat. The financial industry spends a lot of money on computer algorithms to predict the future. A lot of money is really an understatement. Why/how is twitter good at predicting the market?
