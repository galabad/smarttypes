At a high level, the following is about reducing the dimensionality of a graph. Detailing the value in graph reduction is a topic i'll leave for another day. Assuming then, the inherit value in graphs. Reducing the dimensionality of a graph (squeezing out it's essence) is also valuable. There a lot of methods of dimension reduction. There are a lot of methods of graph analysis. The method/model described here uses N-body simulation to reduce a graph to 2 dimensions. Reducing the dimensionality of our graph this much has the nice effect of making it human digestible.

We're interested in a 2d graph layout. Stay w/ me for a second. We're going to scatter all the nodes in our graph on a 2d surface. Our nodes interact w/ each other until they settle down to some kind of equilibrium. This is know generally as Energy Minimization, and is commonly used to model complex physical systems. Fans of complexity and social science will see a lot of similarities w/ this and Agent Based Modeling.

Lets talk for a minute about our goal. I'm interested in graph clustering.
